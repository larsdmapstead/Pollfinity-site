How We Compute It

Weighting Methodology

Three-Factor Weighting System

Each poll receives a combined weight equal to the product of three factors: pollster quality tier (1.0 for top-rated firms like YouGov, Ipsos, and Quinnipiac; down to 0.3 for unrated sources), recency (1.0 for polls from the last 3 days, declining to 0.2 for polls older than 14 days), and sample size (√(n/1000), so a 1,000-person poll scores 1.0 and a 4,000-person poll scores ~2.0).

The final weighted average is the sum of (value × weight) divided by the sum of all weights — a standard weighted mean. Only polls from the last 30 days are included in current averages; historical trend lines use 90-day windows in 3-day buckets.

📬 Are You a Polling Organization?

If your firm conducts public opinion research and you'd like your polls included in the Pollfinity weighted average, we'd love to hear from you. We accept:

  • Topline and crosstab PDFs — email directly to polls@pollfinity.com
  • RSS feeds — we'll add your feed to our daily ingestion pipeline
  • API access — we support custom integrations for structured data
  • Press list inclusion — get added to our media distribution for future releases

Inclusion is free. We cite every pollster by name and link to the original release. Contact us at polls@pollfinity.com with the subject line "Pollster Inclusion Request."
